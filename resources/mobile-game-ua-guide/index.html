The Ultimate Guide to Mobile Game User Acquisition (UA) for Indie Developers

NL

Mobile Growth

You built a great game. The UA bill is about to humble you. Mobile game user acquisition is the discipline of finding players who will actually pay (or watch ads) — at a cost lower than what they're worth to you. Get that math right and you have a business. Get it wrong and you have an expensive hobby.

This guide covers every channel, metric, and creative decision you need to run UA profitably — without a $50k/month agency or a war chest from a publisher.

Executive Summary
  • The only metric that matters is LTV:CPI ratio. If a player costs you more to acquire than they generate over their lifetime, you are paying to lose. Every tactical decision in this guide flows from that one equation.
  • Playable ads outperform video and static on mobile — 2–5× higher CVR — because they filter for intent before the install. Start there.
  • Cross-promotion is the most underused, lowest-cost UA channel for developers with more than one game. A mutual discovery network (like Noga Labs) eliminates the media buy entirely.

1. The Math Every UA Manager Must Know

What is CPI, LTV, and ROAS — and how do they connect?

Every UA campaign lives or dies on three numbers:

MetricDefinitionFormula
CPI (Cost Per Install)What you pay per new playerTotal ad spend ÷ Total installs
LTV (Lifetime Value)Revenue a player generates over their entire time in your gameARPU × Average session lifespan
ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)How much revenue you earn per dollar spentTotal revenue from campaign ÷ Total ad spend

How to calculate if a campaign is profitable

The core equation:

LTV:CPI Ratio = LTV / CPI

Break-even:        LTV:CPI = 1.0   → you recover what you spent
Minimum viable:    LTV:CPI = 1.3   → 30% margin to cover costs
Healthy:           LTV:CPI ≥ 2.0   → sustainable scale

Example:
  LTV = $2.00   CPI = $1.20
  LTV:CPI = 2.00 / 1.20 = 1.67  ✓ viable

Why D1, D7, and D30 retention are your leading indicators

You won't know true LTV for months. Retention rates are your early signal:

BenchmarkCasualMid-CoreHard-Core
D1 Retention35–45%30–40%25–35%
D7 Retention12–18%15–22%18–28%
D30 Retention4–8%6–12%10–18%
If your D1 is below 30%, fix the game before scaling UA. Paid acquisition amplifies retention problems — it doesn't solve them.

2. How to Choose UA Channels

Which UA channel is right for your game's stage?

Pre-launch / Soft launch$0–$5k/moASO, organic social, cross-promo
Early scale$5k–$25k/moMeta Advantage+, Google UAC, playable ad networks
Growth$25k–$100k/moAdd ironSource, AppLovin, Unity Ads, retargeting
Scale$100k+/moFull-funnel: paid + influencer + programmatic

How to evaluate a new UA channel in 3 steps

  1. Run a small test budget (10–15% of monthly spend) for 14 days minimum — algorithms need time to exit the learning phase.
  2. Compare blended CPI against your target. Never judge a channel on the first week; Meta and Google campaigns typically drop CPI 20–35% between day 7 and day 21 as the algorithm optimises.
  3. Check install quality, not just volume. A channel delivering $0.30 CPI but 2% D1 retention is worse than $1.50 CPI with 40% D1 retention.

3. Paid UA: Meta, Google UAC, and Ad Networks

How does Meta Advantage+ work for mobile games?

Meta's Advantage+ app campaigns are fully automated — you provide creatives, a budget, and a target event (install, registration, purchase), and Meta's algorithm handles audience targeting. Key settings:

  • Campaign objective: App installs or App events (use App events once you have 50+ conversions/week)
  • Budget type: Daily budget, not lifetime — easier to scale and pause
  • Creative supply: Upload 10–15 creatives per campaign. Meta rotates and auto-optimises. Replace the bottom 20% performers weekly.
  • Attribution window: Use 7-day click / 1-day view for mobile games

Meta CPI benchmarks (2026):

Mid-core RPG$2.50–$6.00
Strategy$3.00–$8.00

How does Google UAC differ from Meta for game UA?

Google Universal App Campaigns source inventory from Search, Play Store, YouTube, and Display Network simultaneously. You cannot choose placements — Google decides.

Meta Advantage+Google UAC

Creative formatsImage, video, carouselImage, video, HTML5 playable
Best forIntent-based discoveryExisting demand capture
Min data for optimisation50 conversions/week100 installs/day
Google-specific tip: HTML5 playable ads get preferential placement in Play Store search results. If you only run playables on one platform, make it Google.

4. Playable Ads: The Highest-Converting Creative Format

Why do playable ads outperform video and static creatives?

Playable ads are interactive HTML5 experiences (15–60 seconds) that let a user try your core mechanic before installing. The performance gap vs. passive formats:

FormatAverage CVRAverage D1 Retention (installs)
Static banner0.5–1.5%Baseline
Video (15s)1.5–3.5%+5–10% vs banner
Playable4–12%+15–25% vs banner

The retention lift is the critical insight. Playable installs self-select — users who install after playing your mechanic already know if they like it.

How to structure a playable ad that converts

Every high-performing playable follows this exact sequence:

  1. Hook (0–3s): Animated hand prompt. No text. One gestural cue only.
  2. Core loop (3–25s): One satisfying completion of your core mechanic. Design for success — users who fail drop off at 3× the rate of users who win.
  3. Win moment (25–35s): Reward animation. This is the emotional peak — the moment that makes the player think "I want more of that."
  4. Endcard (35s+): Full-screen install CTA with app icon, star rating, and store badge. Loop the playable in the background if no tap within 3 seconds.
Critical constraint: Total asset size must be under 2MB. Load time above 3 seconds loses 60%+ of users before they interact.

5. Cross-Promotion and Mutual Discovery Networks

What is cross-promotion between games and why is it underrated?

Cross-promotion is showing ads for one of your games inside another game you own. For a developer with 2+ titles, it's the lowest-cost UA channel available — your marginal cost is zero, since you're using existing ad inventory.

Cross-promotion economics:

Effective CPI from cross-promo = $0

Value transferred = (LTV of cross-promoted install) × (% who install)

Example:
  10,000 DAU × 2% CTR × 15% CVR = 300 installs/day at $0 cost
  vs $1.50 CPI paid elsewhere → $450/day saved

What is a mutual discovery network and how does it expand cross-promo beyond your own portfolio?

A mutual discovery network extends cross-promotion to games you don't own. Developers agree to show each other's playable ads — you run their ads in your game, they run yours. No money changes hands; impressions are exchanged.

Traditional Paid UAMutual Discovery Network
Cost$1–$8 CPI$0 (impression barter)
Audience qualityAlgorithm-matchedProven mobile gamers
Setup time1–2 weeksHours
Minimum budget$5,000+/moNone

6. Organic UA: ASO, Influencers, and Community

How to optimise your App Store listing (ASO) to increase organic installs

App Store Optimisation (ASO) is SEO for app stores. Ranking higher in search results is free installs. The 4 elements that drive ASO ranking:

  1. App title and subtitle: Include your primary keyword in the title. Apple indexes the title heavily; Google less so.
  2. Keyword field (iOS only): 100-character field. No spaces — use commas. Include secondary terms not in your title/description.
  3. Conversion rate of your listing page: A/B test your icon, screenshots, and preview video. A 1% lift in store CVR compounds across all traffic sources.
  4. Ratings velocity: Apps with 4.4+ stars and 1,000+ recent ratings rank meaningfully higher. Prompt happy players (post-win moments) to rate.
ASO benchmark to aim for: Top 10 keyword ranking in your genre drives 15–40% of total installs for successful indie titles.

When do influencers and content creators make sense for indie UA?

Influencer UA works when your game has a strong visual hook (watchable gameplay), LTV is high enough to absorb $500–$5,000+ per sponsored video, and you have a clear attribution method (promo codes, UTM links, or installs spike tracking).

Avoid influencer UA before you have a D1 retention of 35%+ — viewer installs churn faster than paid installs, and a surge of low-quality installs will tank your store ranking.

7. Creative Strategy and Testing Framework

How to build a creative testing pipeline

  1. Generate 10–15 concepts per creative cycle based on competitor analysis, player reviews, and top-performing moments in your game.
  2. Produce lightweight versions first — a static mock, a rough cut, a simple playable prototype. Test with $500–$1,000 before full production.
  3. Declare a winner at statistical significance — minimum 1,000 impressions per variant, minimum 50 conversion events. Use a 95% confidence threshold.
  4. Iterate the winner — change one variable at a time (background colour, hook, win condition). Never change two variables simultaneously.
  5. Retire creatives at fatigue — watch for CPM rising >20% vs launch week. That's creative fatigue. Retire and replace.

8. How to Set and Manage a UA Budget

How much should an indie developer spend on UA?

Budget should be derived from your unit economics, not from what you can afford:

Maximum viable daily spend = (Target installs/day) × (Max CPI)

Max CPI = LTV × (1 / Target LTV:CPI ratio)

Example:
  LTV = $2.00,  Target ratio = 1.5
  Max CPI = $2.00 / 1.5 = $1.33
  Target 200 installs/day
  Max daily budget = 200 × $1.33 = $266/day

Rules for scaling:

  • Only increase daily budget by 20–30% at a time — larger jumps reset algorithm learning phases
  • Wait 5–7 days between budget increases to let the algorithm restabilise
  • Never scale a campaign with fewer than 50 conversions/week; the data is too thin to optimise from

9. Measuring What Matters: Your KPI Stack

What KPIs should indie UA managers track daily vs. weekly vs. monthly?

CadenceKPIs to track
Daily CPI by campaign and creative · Installs by channel · D1 retention (7-day rolling avg) · Spend pacing
Weekly D7 retention cohort · ROAS D7 by channel · Creative fatigue (CPM trend) · LTV:CPI by cohort
Monthly D30 retention and LTV · Blended ROAS · Payback period · Channel diversification ratio

What is payback period and why does it matter for indie developers?

Payback period (months) = CPI / Monthly ARPU

Example:
  CPI = $2.00,  Monthly ARPU = $0.40
  Payback = 2.00 / 0.40 = 5 months

Healthy range for indie mobile games:  6–12 months
Danger zone:                           >18 months
(cash flow will kill you before LTV arrives)
