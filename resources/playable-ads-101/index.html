Playable Ads 101: What They Are and Why They Work

NL
Noga Labs Team
Mobile Growth
 Jun 23, 2026 8 min read

Playable ads are interactive mini-experiences that let a potential player try a snippet of your game before they download it. Unlike a static banner or a 15-second video, a playable ad puts the controls in the user's hands — and that changes everything about how UA performs.

What exactly is a playable ad?

A playable ad is a self-contained HTML5 experience — typically 15 to 60 seconds long — that runs inside an ad slot on networks like Meta, Google UAC, ironSource, or AppLovin. The user taps, drags, or interacts with a simplified version of your game mechanic. At the end, they're shown a call-to-action to install.

They became mainstream around 2018 when platforms started supporting MRAID and HTML5 ad formats at scale, but they've become the dominant creative format for mobile games in the past two years — particularly for hyper-casual and puzzle genres.

Key stat: Playable ads consistently achieve 2–5× higher conversion rates than equivalent video ads for mobile games, according to ironSource and AppLovin network data.

How do they differ from video ads?

Video ads are passive — the user watches. Playable ads are active — the user plays. That difference drives three measurable outcomes:

  • Higher intent installs. Users who choose to install after playing your mechanic already know whether they like it. Day-1 retention is typically 10–20% higher for playable-sourced installs versus video-sourced installs.
  • Better ROAS. Because you're filtering for engaged users upfront, your downstream monetisation events (IAP, ad views) are more likely to fire.
  • Creative data. Every tap, swipe, and point of drop-off in a playable ad is a data point. You can see exactly where users lose interest — something a video creative can never tell you.

The anatomy of a good playable ad

Most high-performing playable ads share the same structure:

  1. Hook (0–3s): An animated prompt — usually a hand icon — shows the user what to do. No text. No explanation. Just a clear visual cue.
  2. Core loop (3–30s): The user performs one satisfying loop of your core mechanic. For a puzzle game, that's one puzzle. For a merge game, that's one merge chain. For a shooter, that's one short combat encounter.
  3. Win moment: The user should succeed. Playable ads that let users fail have significantly higher drop-off rates. Design the experience so success is achievable in under 30 seconds.
  4. Endcard: A full-screen CTA with your game icon, store rating, and an install button. Optionally loop back to the playable if the user doesn't tap.

Common mistakes to avoid

After reviewing hundreds of playable ad creatives, these are the mistakes that consistently hurt performance:

  • Too much tutorial. If you need more than one tap to explain what to do, it's too complex. Simplify the mechanic.
  • Replicating the full game. The playable ad is a hook, not a demo. Show the most satisfying five seconds of your game, not a representative cross-section.
  • Slow load time. Anything over 3 seconds to interactive and you've already lost most users. Compress assets aggressively — aim for under 2MB total.
  • Mismatched expectations. The playable ad mechanic should closely mirror the actual game. "Bait and switch" playables get installs but destroy Day-7 retention.
