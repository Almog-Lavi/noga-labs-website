## Tone of Voice

### What we are
- A **product** — not a brand campaign
- Focused on **what the tool does**, not who the competition is
- Direct and plain — complexity is the enemy

### Voice principles
- **Clear over clever** — if it needs explaining, rewrite it
- **Specific over vague** — "build demos in minutes" beats "streamline your workflow"
- **Active over passive** — "earn tokens" not "tokens are earned"
- **Short sentences win** — especially for hero copy

### Words to avoid
| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| immersive | engaging, playable |
| leverage | use |
| cutting-edge | (only use if specific) |
| seamless | (overused — be specific) |
| revolutionize | (hyperbolic) |
| reduced risk | lower risk / without the risk |
| exchanging | earning and spending |
| solution | (vague — name the actual thing) |

### Words we like
- playable, engage, convert, install, earn, spend, credits, demos, mobile, team, budget

## What This Product Is (Reference for all copy)

Use this as the north star when generating any new copy:

> Noga Labs is a platform for mobile game developers — especially small teams with limited budget and no time — that lets them:
> 1. **Create playable demos** quickly, without needing a dedicated dev team
> 2. **Advertise using earned credits** (a credit exchange marketplace), reducing the need for cash ad spend

Both benefits are equally important and should be represented whenever space allows.

---

## What We Do NOT Do

- We do not position against big studios or large teams
- We do not use fear, urgency, or scarcity as a primary message (the waitlist "early bird" reward is the one exception, and it's soft)
- We do not make promises about specific numbers or results
- We do not use first-person plural ("we built this because...") in hero copy — keep it product-focused
